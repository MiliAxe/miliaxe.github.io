LLMs have clearly been the hype for a while now. We see individuals following
LLMs blindly. Especially in the domain of making Software. I have always
had this very defiant outlook towards them, probably because I thought
moving against the crowd is a good idea and makes me stand out.

Since I have started my journey on attempting to enter academia, i.e.
trying my best to start doing research. I have been struggling with
reading papers. And I have found using an LLM (namely, Gemini 2.5 Pro) wisely
and cautiously can be very helpful.

## My initial hate towards LLMs

I have always felt some sort of "guilt" while using LLMs. As if using them
is cheating somehow. To some, it might feel offensive to get help from an
LLM:

> I don't need to get help from a clanker. They are inaccurate, and they always
> spit garbage output.

Well, this is quite correct. But I would say calling them "always inaccurate"
would be quite a bit of an overstatement. Specially with the top ranking ones.

I mean, if you don't really need to use LLMs regularly and can read
and understand everything quickly. then, good news for you! You are a very
intellectually capable human.

But I have recently started to think of talking to an LLM from another view point:

**Imagine you are chatting with a random stranger off the street that you
have no clue whether they are right or not.**

Thinking of it this way, makes it... a little bit less hateful. I have
started to think of them as a person who is very likely to make
mistakes while talking.
